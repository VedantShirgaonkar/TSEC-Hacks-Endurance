# Office Memorandum
## Work From Home (WFH) Policy

**No.**: A-11013/2/2023-Estt  
**Date**: 15th February 2023

---

## Subject: Guidelines for Work From Home (WFH) facility for officers and staff of the Department

In exercise of the powers conferred under Rule 11 of the Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, the following guidelines are hereby issued for regulating the Work From Home facility in the Department of Information Technology.

---

## 1. Applicability

This policy is applicable to:
- All officers of Level 6 and above
- Staff with minimum 2 years of service in the Department
- Employees with no pending disciplinary action

**Not Applicable to:**
- Officers handling classified/secret documents
- Employees in probation period
- Staff below Level 6 (except with special approval)

---

## 2. Eligibility Criteria

| Category | WFH Days Allowed (per week) |
|----------|----------------------------|
| Joint Secretary and above | 1 day |
| Director/Deputy Secretary | 2 days |
| Under Secretary/Section Officer | 2 days |
| Below US level (with approval) | 1 day |

---

## 3. Core Working Requirements

### 3.1 Core Hours
- **Mandatory availability**: 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM
- Officers must be reachable via official email and phone during core hours

### 3.2 Technical Requirements
- Stable internet connection (minimum 10 Mbps)
- VPN connectivity to official network
- Laptop/desktop with updated security software
- Official email access

---

## 4. Application Process

1. Submit WFH request to Reporting Officer **minimum 3 days in advance**
2. Reporting Officer approval required before availing WFH
3. Emergency WFH can be approved same-day with justification
4. Maximum 8 WFH days per month (excluding emergencies)

---

## 5. Restrictions

WFH is **NOT permitted** during:
- Parliament sessions (for relevant sections)
- VIP visits and official meetings
- Audit periods
- Last week of financial year (March 25-31)

---

## 6. Monitoring and Compliance

- Daily work report to be submitted by 6:00 PM on WFH days
- Random calls may be made to verify availability
- Non-compliance will result in WFH privileges being revoked
- Repeated violations may lead to disciplinary action
